## Full script with scene markers

<S1>
  Out in the field, the work that powers America is still being run on whiteboards, group texts, and the back of a fuel receipt.

  <S2>
  Jobs get missed. Paperwork disappears. Vendors sit waiting on a call that never comes. Owners chase status updates from foremen who are already three sites away. And every month, a fortune in work gets billed late, billed wrong, or never billed at all.

  <S3>
  Meet VNDRLY — pronounced "vendor-lee." One platform. Office and field. Web and mobile. Every job, every crew, every dollar — on the record.

  <S4>
  VNDRLY connects the four people who actually run a job: Partners who own the work, Vendors who bid it, Field Employees who execute it, and VNDRLY Admins who keep the rails clean. Everyone sees what they need. Nobody sees what they shouldn't.

  <S5>
  Every site location is mapped, geofenced, and tied to its AFE code — so every minute, every mile, and every part rolls up against the right cost center automatically. No more guessing where the spend went.

  <S6>
  A shared master catalog defines exactly what work can be done at each site, and the negotiated price each vendor has contracted with each partner to do it. Quotes stop being arguments. They become lookups.

  <S7>
  This is the Hotlist. When a partner has time-sensitive work, it goes up here. Approved vendors see it instantly and bid in seconds. Unapproved vendors can watch — they just can't bid yet. The moment an award goes out, the hotlist post becomes a live ticket, and the vendor's crew is already scheduling the truck.

  <S8>
  Becoming an approved vendor used to take weeks. With VNDRLY's onboarding tool, it's bite-sized: upload your COI, negotiate your catalog pricing, sign the contract, set up your crews — all in chunks you can finish between jobs.

  <S9>
  Dispatching is one click. The ticket lands on the vendor's mobile app with the site, the scope, the AFE, and the agreed price already attached.

  <S10>
  Then the field takes over. Accept. En route — with live GPS. On location — verified by the site geofence. In progress, with photos and notes streaming back to the office. Mileage captured on the drive in and the drive out. Day-rate or hourly labor, with tax automatically calculated. Completed and submitted, ready for partner approval — all without a single phone call.

  <S11>
  Foremen manage their crews from the same screen — checking guys in, capturing time, and tracking compliance cards: Safety, Certifications, Training. If somebody's cert is about to expire, VNDRLY flags it before they ever set foot on a rig.

  <S12>
  Parts and Labor write themselves. Labor auto-fills on checkout. Parts pull from the catalog. Equipment gets logged once and rides every ticket it touches.

  <S13>
  At the gate, the Visitors Log captures everyone who comes and goes — and the partner's Site Map shows every active crew and visitor in real time.

  <S14>
  At month-end, accounting hits one button. Tickets roll into invoices. Invoices export straight into QuickBooks, billed by AFE code, with reports your CFO will actually read.

  <S15>
  And the analytics layer turns all of it into signal — which vendors hit on time, which jobs blow budget, which sites consume the most labor — so partners stop reacting and start steering.

  <S16>
  SOC 2 ready. End-to-end audit trail. Real customers. Real jobs. Real numbers. VNDRLY. Your field, on the record. Visit vndrly dot ai.
